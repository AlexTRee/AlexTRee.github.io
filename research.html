1. [NGS Analysis]

The systemic shifts in miRNA landcape as a diagnostic and prediction tool



The new high-throughput next-generation sequencing (NGS) technologies greatly changed the research approaches in many fields, including cancer genomics. The high sensitivity and specificity of NGS significantly facilitates studies of miRNAs, small non-coding RNA molecules that regulate expression of many protein-coding genes. With NGS, both the abundance profiling of miRNAs in the sample, and the discovery of novel miRNA species is achieved. By comparing miRNA expression profiles between samples, global features of gene expression regulation landscape can be extracted, and used to infer the physiologically relevant information.

2. [Phylogenetic Systematics Analysis]

Genomics and evolution of human adenoviruses (HAdV)

Several members of the adenoviruses family are important human pathogens. There are 67 published HAdV types in species A-G, most of which are agents in acute respiratory disease (ARD) and many other diseases. Our joint project with JCVI of analyzing 55 newly sequenced HAdV species A, B and C shows using new bioinformatics tools and whole-genome sequence need to be considered while characterize and name HAdV. Since there are many interspecies recombination events are observed (eg. human vs chimpanzee), new phylogenetic trees are proposed and constructed.

















3. [Metagenomics Analysis]

Metagenomics analysis of Bacterial & Archaeal abundances from environment samples.

Bacterial abundances, archaeal abundances with acetate production, biogas and CH4/CO2 composition from various coal samples and cyanobacterial mats (hot spring samples, and Black Band disease samples) was analyzed. Canonical correlation analysis seeks to find linear combinations of variables in one data set and linear combinations of variables in a second (corresponding) data set that together explain the maximum amount of variation between samples.















4. [Development of Bioinformatics Tools]

Genome Navigator - A NGS Visualization Tool



Genome Navigator (GN) is a unique Java-based visualization tool for analyzing NGS data which is easy to be integrated into most web browsers through an operating system independent manner. GN is distinctive in handling large NGS datasets, finding interesting regions, and validating user's hypotheses. GN supports data stream from both local and remote sources with the user interaction and customization. The highlighted colors enable users to examine the variations, insertions, and deletions at desired genomic resolution level.
