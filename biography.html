Professional Short Bio

Tiange Cui is currently working on his PhD of Bioinformatics & Computational Biology in the School of Systems Biology at George Mason University. His principal research interests lie in the development of next-generation sequencing (NGS) data analysis algorithms and tools using popular programming languages and statistical methodology, and analysing high-throughput (such as WGS, WES, microarray, RNA-seq, ChIP-seq) dataset with summarizing and visualizing results, for solving problems involving characterization of gene expression profile(GEP), structural variant(SV), SNPs, copy number variants (CNV), genome annotation and classification, and computational meta'omics analysis for microbiome. Tiange Cui's long-term pursuit is to understand complex human diseases by undertaking integrative approaches combining Biology, Bioinformatics, Statistics, and Omics. Tiange Cui received M.S. in Biology from Rutgers University, and B.E. in Bioengineering from Wuhan Institute of Technology. His current work involves, 1) The systemic shifts in miRNA landscape as a diagnostic and prediction tool by using distance-based classifiers; 2) Using new bioinformatics tools to annotate and characterize Human Adenovirus (HAdVs) and its evolution. 3) Metagenomics analysis of Bacterial & Archaeal abundances from environment samples.

A little more about me

I was born in Shijiazhuang, Hebei, China and spend most of my adolescent life there. After completing my B.E degree major in Bioengineering in Wuhan Institute of Technology, China, I came to the United States in 2009 and studied the developmental Biology (Cell signalling, tissue patterning and morphogenesis during fruit fly oogenesis) at Rutgers University, New Jersey, under Dr. Nir Yakoby. I received my M.S degree in Biology in May 2012, I worked as a research assistant at Rutgers' Center for Computational and Integrative Biology, New Jersey, under Dr. Andrey Grigoriev, during which time I redeveloped a Java-based genome visualization tool - 'derBrowser', and found my passion in utilizing computational tools to analysis biological data especially sequencing data, rather than via pure experimental approach. I moved on in 2013 and started my PhD study in Bioinformatics & Computational Biology at George Mason University, Virginia.

In my spare time, I enjoy playing basketball, outdoor jogging, travelling, watching non-mainstream movies, listening to alternative, britpop and jazz music, reading deep novels, and photographing precious moments. I have inexhaustible passion for new things, technologies and places. I always try my personal best to keep myself updated both mentally and physically. I love beautiful people and amazing stories. But the most important person in my life is my wife, Winna Xi, who means the whole world to me.
